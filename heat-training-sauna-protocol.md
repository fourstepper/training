# Heat Training & Sauna Protocol for Endurance Athletes

*Research summary and practical recommendations - December 10, 2025*

---

## Key Study: Long-term Passive Heat Acclimation

**Citation:** Jenkins et al. (2025). "Long‐term passive heat acclimation enhances maximal oxygen consumption via haematological and cardiac adaptation in endurance runners." *The Journal of Physiology*. DOI: 10.1113/JP289874

### Main Findings

- Long-term passive heat acclimation (e.g., hot-water immersion, sauna) can enhance VO2max in endurance runners
- Improvements occur through both haematological (increased hemoglobin mass) and cardiac adaptations
- Passive strategies allow athletes to maintain normal training intensities while still getting adaptation benefits
- Positioned as a potential alternative to altitude training

### Why This Matters

1. **Accessibility:** No need to travel to altitude or use expensive simulation equipment
2. **Training Quality:** Unlike active heat training, passive heat doesn't compromise workout quality
3. **Practical:** Can be done with a hot bath or sauna

## Research-Based Sauna Protocol

### Initial Acclimation Phase (2-3 weeks)

**Optimal Parameters:**
- **Frequency:** 3-4 sessions per week
- **Duration:** 30-40 minutes per session
- **Temperature:** 80-90°C (sauna)
- **Timing:** Post-exercise timing is optimal but not required (see Timing Considerations below)
- **Minimum:** 6-7 exposures to induce initial adaptation

**Key Study Result:** 12 sauna sessions at 90°C for 30 minutes post-exercise over 21 days resulted in a **32% improvement** in running time to exhaustion.

### Maintenance Phase (Long-term)

**Recommended:**
- **Frequency:** 2x per week
- **Duration:** 30-40 minutes
- **Evidence:** Research confirms twice-weekly sessions can maintain physiological adaptations after initial acclimation

---

## Physiological Benefits

### Cardiovascular Adaptations
- **Plasma volume expansion:** 5-20% increase
- **Enhanced cardiac output:** More blood pumped per beat
- **Improved oxygen delivery:** To working muscles

### Performance Improvements
- Reduced core temperature during exercise
- Improved thermoregulation
- Lower blood lactate levels
- Increased skeletal muscle force
- Enhanced mitochondrial function
- **1.9% improvement** in endurance time trials (documented)

### Important Note
These adaptations improve performance in **both hot AND temperate conditions** - not just for racing in heat.

## Practical Implementation

### 2x Per Week Protocol Assessment

**For Initial Adaptation:**
- Suboptimal but better than nothing
- Expect ~10-15% less adaptation compared to 3-4x per week
- Will still produce beneficial adaptations, just slower

**For Maintenance:**
- Solid and research-backed
- Sufficient to maintain adaptations long-term
- Practical for busy training schedules

### Recommended Approach

**Option 1 (Optimal):**
1. Initial block: 3-4x per week for 2-3 weeks
2. Maintenance: Drop to 2x per week indefinitely

**Option 2 (Practical):**
- Start and maintain 2x per week
- Consistency over perfection
- Better to do 2x per week consistently than aim for 4x and burn out

### Timing Considerations

**Post-exercise timing (optimal):**
- Produces superior adaptations due to synergistic stress response
- Most research protocols used immediate post-exercise timing
- Complete your regular training first, then enter sauna for 30-40 minutes

**Standalone sessions (still effective):**
- Can successfully induce heat adaptations and improve performance
- Studies show passive heating alone produces plasma volume expansion and cardiovascular benefits
- Benefits may be 10-20% less pronounced than post-exercise timing
- Better to do standalone sessions consistently than miss post-exercise timing frequently

**Bottom line on timing:** Post-exercise is optimal but not required. Consistency matters more than perfect timing. Choose the approach that fits your schedule and allows for regular sessions.

## Protocol Details

### Session Structure

**Post-exercise protocol (optimal):**
1. Complete regular training session
2. Brief cool-down (5-10 minutes)
3. Enter sauna at 80-90°C
4. Stay for 30-40 minutes
5. Rehydrate immediately after

**Standalone protocol (effective alternative):**
1. Choose a time when you're well-rested (not immediately pre-workout)
2. Ensure good hydration beforehand
3. Enter sauna at 80-90°C
4. Stay for 30-40 minutes
5. Rehydrate immediately after

### Safety Considerations
- Stay well-hydrated before, during (if possible), and after
- Start conservatively (20-25 minutes) and build up
- Listen to your body
- Exit if you feel dizzy or unwell
- Consider electrolyte supplementation

## Bottom Line

**For someone starting out:**
- 2x per week will provide benefits but slower than 3-4x per week
- Consider pushing to 3-4x during initial 2-3 week block if possible
- Then maintain with 2x per week long-term
- Consistency is more important than perfection
- Benefits apply to all conditions, not just hot weather racing
- **Timing flexibility:** Post-exercise is optimal but standalone sessions are still effective - choose what fits your schedule

**Expected timeline for adaptations:**
- Plasma volume increases: 7-14 days
- Full cardiovascular adaptations: 2-3 weeks
- Maintenance: Ongoing with 2x per week
